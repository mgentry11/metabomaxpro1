The HIT Methodology

High-Intensity Training emphasizes quality over quantity. Each exercise is performed for a single set with slow, controlled movements, maintaining constant tension on the muscle until complete failure is reached. This approach maximizes the growth stimulus while minimizing cumulative fatigue and recovery demands.

Extended Time Under Tension: 90 seconds total per exercise (30s eccentric + 20s concentric + 40s final eccentric)
Complete Muscular Failure: Each set continues until you physically cannot complete another repetition with proper form
Minimal Volume, Maximum Intensity: One properly executed set per exercise provides sufficient stimulus for growth
Adequate Recovery: 2-3 workouts per week with 48-72 hours between sessions allows complete recovery and adaptation

The Protocol Principles

One Set to Failure

Each exercise is performed for a single set taken to complete muscular failure. This maximizes the growth stimulus while minimizing fatigue and recovery demands.

Controlled Tempo

30-second eccentric (lowering), 20-second concentric (lifting), 40-second final eccentric. This extended time under tension is the key to HIT's effectiveness.

Alternating Routines

Alternate between Workout A and B, 2-3 times per week. This provides complete recovery while hitting all major muscle groups twice weekly.

Progressive Resistance

Increase weight by 5-10% when you can complete the full set (90 seconds total time under tension) with good form. Steady, sustainable progress.
