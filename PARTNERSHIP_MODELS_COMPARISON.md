## üéØ RECOMMENDED STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Direct-to-Consumer First (Months 1-3)

**Why start direct:**
1. **Validate pricing** - Is $69 the right price point?
2. **Learn customer needs** - What features matter?
3. **Build testimonials** - Social proof for partnerships
4. **Refine product** - Direct feedback loop
5. **Establish brand** - MetaboMaxPro becomes known
6. **Create leverage** - "We have 100 customers, want to partner?"

**Action items:**
- Launch at $69 direct
- Focus all marketing on consumers
- Build case studies and testimonials
- Track which testing facilities customers use
- Reach out to facilities after 10+ referrals

---

### Phase 2: Add Partner Channel (Months 3-6)

**When you have:**
- 50-100 direct customers
- 5-10 strong testimonials
- Clear facility referral data
- Proven product-market fit

**Then offer partnerships:**

**Partnership Tier 1: Referral Only**
```

```

**Partnership Tier 2: Wholesale**


## üö® CRITICAL CONSIDERATION: AVOID THIS

**Don't Make This Mistake:**

‚ùå **Starting wholesale-only with no direct channel**

**Why this fails:**
1. You don't own customer relationship
2. You can't validate pricing independently
3. No direct feedback for product improvement
4. Facilities negotiate hard on price (race to bottom)
5. If partner churns, you lose all their customers
6. Can't build subscription business
7. Lower total revenue potential

**Famous Example:**
Many SaaS companies tried "reseller-only" and failed because:
- Resellers demanded 50-70% margins
- Product development slowed (no direct feedback)
- Couldn't pivot quickly
- Got commoditized

**Better approach:**
Start direct, add wholesale later with leverage
